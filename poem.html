"Love, the sun"

Love is like sunrise
Rises mysteriously
Like the Chwezi dynasty
Leaving us wondering
How it came about
Love is like the sun
It's morning is all we adore ,embrace
But as it keeps on shinning
It hurts like toothache
Burns like fire flames
And wish it rains
Love is like the sun
When it starts to set
That's when we see its beauty
We crave
Call it all sorts of sweet names
"The golden hours "
Twilight
But when all is for nothing

By Aidah Nabakka

"For a woman"


Life is meaningless
With no child,
Like a body with no soul
Discriminated
Like a victim of leprosy
Seen as a curse
Burden,
Nobody would bother to lift
Harrassed like a slave
Society has no sympathy for her
Ostracized in shame
Yet childless wasn't her will
Cries to the heavens
Tomorrow , please don't come
My little world I've been suppressed
Thrown all around in the circles of arrogant men
Throwing insults at me
Every morning is greeted
With the usual abuses from women,
filling my soul with grief
My hands hunt to hold
Feel ,the heart beat
Of my little one
It's name all over my lips
It's face in my eyes
If this won't happen
Tomorrow , please don't come
I'll rather go along with today
Than live a tomorrow that'll break me conpletely
By Aidah Nabakka

"Pen"


Everytime a nib touches
me i start sparkling
Liveliness and happiness
Fills ,making an ocean
As if am reading a
comic. Your soul is dragged down
Like rain
From a reservoir
To the centre of the nib
Gently like dew
Slipping down
From a leaf
At a carefully controlled rate
Leaving artistic incredible
Markes of a pigment
Filled with love and hope
Which always pulls me,
from just being a piece of paper
To an important document
Thanks alot pen,
Without you am nothing
By Aidah Nabakka

"Wild flower"


Been blown east to west
Like the waters of the
ocean. Looking for a path
To make my dream a reality
Drowned in a pool of
pain. As everyone sees my dream
Unreal as a myth
Draining all my hope
Been undermined
Because of my front page
No,no is the song
On everyone's lips i ran to
Lonely as a wildflower
Gathered my roots
Supported myself
So as to bloom
Show them the gold they missed
A star i became
When the left me in the darkness
By Aidah Nabakka
